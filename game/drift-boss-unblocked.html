Drive Until You Fall Off

The track floats in the sky with no guardrails. Sharp corners, ramps, narrow sections, and the occasional jump appear as you drive forward automatically. Tap or click to turn right. Release to go left. Miss a corner by holding too long or releasing too early and the car goes over the edge. The scoring is based on distance. Coins collect during runs and unlock new vehicles. No finish line. No levels. Just how far you can go before falling.

Vehicles and What They Do

The default car handles in a predictable way that's good for learning the track feel. Unlockable vehicles include trucks, tanks, and buses. Heavier vehicles handle slightly differently on corners. A bus corners slower than a sports car, which changes the timing of every tap. Most players stick to lighter vehicles until they understand the base mechanics, then experiment with heavier ones for variety.

How to Stop Falling Off the Track

  • Tap, don't hold: Most new players hold too long and overshoot corners. Short taps give more control than long holds. The car responds faster than most players expect.
  • Watch the track ahead: Look two corners ahead, not at the current corner. Reacting to what's right in front of you is already too late at faster speeds.
  • Ramps need early input: Before a ramp, tap slightly earlier than you think you need to. The car takes a moment to respond and late input on a ramp sends you off the side.
  • Use boosters strategically: Car Insurance gives you one extra chance after falling. Save it for runs where you've already gotten far rather than using it on early falls.
  • Daily rewards worth checking: Logging in daily gives bonus coins. Coins unlock vehicles and boosters faster. A few daily check-ins add up quickly.
