Monkey Mart Unblocked: Running a Supermarket Has No Right Being This Addictive

You're a monkey. You run a grocery store. You harvest bananas, stock shelves, ring up customers. That's it. Monkey Mart Unblocked sounds like it should last 10 minutes. Spent 35 minutes on it testing the page and forgot to check the time. The loop is satisfying in a way that's hard to explain until you're deep in your third aisle expansion wondering where the break went.

What You're Actually Doing

You control a monkey moving around a supermarket. Walk near a crop station and the monkey harvests automatically. Walk near an empty shelf and it restocks automatically. Walk near a customer at the checkout and the transaction happens automatically. The game is built around routing. You figure out the most efficient path through your store to keep everything running without customers waiting too long. Early on you do everything yourself. Later you hire assistant monkeys to handle specific stations while you expand.

Your Monkey Runs the Show

You start with bananas and corn. As you earn money, new product categories unlock: eggs, popcorn, coffee, chocolate. Each new product requires its own station, its own shelf space, and its own harvest cycle. The store grows from a small two-aisle operation into something that takes actual planning to run efficiently. The assistant monkeys you hire help but they're not smart enough to cover everything on their own. You're always the most important worker in the building.

Controls

  • WASD or Arrow Keys: Move your monkey around the store
  • Automatic: Harvesting, restocking, and checkout all happen when you walk near the right spot
  • Mouse: Click menus to upgrade stations and hire staff

Getting the Store Running Efficiently

  • Prioritize the checkout first: Customers waiting to pay is the fastest way to lose satisfaction. Always pass by the register before heading to harvest or restock.
  • Hire an assistant for bananas early: Bananas sell the fastest and run out quickest. Getting an assistant on that station frees you to manage everything else.
  • Don't expand too fast: Adding a new product before you have enough staff for existing ones creates chaos. Add one section at a time, hire staff, stabilize, then expand.
  • Upgrade station speed before shelf size: Faster production matters more than bigger shelves early. Shelves that refill slowly are the bottleneck most players miss.
  • Watch customer patience meters: The bar above each customer shows how long they'll wait. Checkout customers have the shortest patience. Prioritize them over restocking.
