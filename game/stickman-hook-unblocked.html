Stickman Hook Unblocked: Spider-Man Physics in a Browser Tab

The pitch for Stickman Hook Unblocked is simple: swing a stickman through colorful obstacle levels using a grappling hook. One button. The execution is satisfying in a way that takes about 10 seconds to understand and significantly longer to master. Spent 30 minutes on it and made it to level 18 before stopping. Came back the next session and went further. The momentum physics reward practice in a way that makes every run feel like progress.

One Hook, One Button, Infinite Ways to Swing

Your stickman moves automatically from left to right. Hold click or Space to fire a rope at the nearest anchor point and swing. Release at the right moment and your stickman launches forward with the momentum you built up. The goal is to reach the finish line without hitting obstacles or falling. Every level adds something new. Moving platforms, spinning barriers, narrow gaps that require precise release timing. 150+ levels and each one feels different from the last.

The Momentum System Nobody Explains

The height you release at determines how far you fly. Releasing at the bottom of your swing sends you forward with maximum speed. Releasing at the top changes your arc entirely. Most players figure this out around level 20 when the gaps get wide enough that basic swinging stops working. The physics engine is consistent, which means once you understand it, the levels open up. What feels impossible at first becomes readable once the momentum logic clicks.

What Separates Good Runs From Crashes

  • Release at the bottom of the swing: Maximum forward momentum comes from releasing when the stickman is at the lowest point of the arc. Most beginners release too early, losing speed.
  • Don't hold too long: Holding past the bottom of the swing starts pulling you backward. Quick holds with precise release beats long holds almost every time.
  • Watch the next anchor point: Look ahead at where you need to attach next, not at where you currently are. Planning two swings ahead prevents the panicked late-attach that causes most crashes.
  • Short swings through tight gaps: In narrow sections, quick short taps work better than full swings. They give more control over exact position.
  • Speed is your friend on wide gaps: For large open sections, build up speed with multiple full swings before attempting the gap. Going too slow drops you short.
