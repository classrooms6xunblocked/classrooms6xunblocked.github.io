Cookie Clicker Unblocked: The Game That Started Idle Gaming in 2013

Orteil coded Cookie Clicker Unblocked in one night and posted it on 4chan in August 2013. It hit 50,000 players within hours and 1.5 million daily visits by the end of that month. The concept is absurd: click a giant cookie. Buy things that click it for you. Repeat until the numbers get ridiculous. Opened it to test the page and looked up 45 minutes later. Still not sure exactly what happened in between.

Click a Cookie. Then Don't.

The game starts with you clicking a large cookie on the left side of the screen. Each click earns one cookie. With enough cookies you can buy a Cursor that clicks automatically. Then a Grandma who bakes cookies. Then a Farm, a Mine, a Factory, a Bank, a Temple, a Wizard Tower, a Shipment, an Alchemy Lab, a Portal, a Time Machine, an Antimatter Condenser, a Prism, a Chancemaker, a Fractal Engine, a Javascript Console, an Idleverse. The production scales from cookies per second into the trillions. Eventually you're producing more cookies per second than have ever existed. There's lore behind all of it and the Grandmas get unsettling.

The Grandmapocalypse Is a Real Thing

If you upgrade your Grandmas enough times, a Grandmapocalypse triggers. The game's visual style changes, Grandmas start muttering ominous things, and wrinklers appear on the main cookie, slowly consuming it but generating extra cookies when popped. Most players don't expect this the first time. It's one of the more creative things hidden in what looks like a simple clicker game.

Controls

  • Left Click (cookie): Produce cookies manually
  • Left Click (upgrades): Purchase upgrades in the top panel
  • Left Click (buildings): Buy production buildings on the right
  • Golden Cookie: Click immediately when it appears. Big bonus

What Actually Moves the Numbers Fast

  • Click Golden Cookies instantly: These appear randomly and disappear quickly. A Golden Cookie can multiply your production for a few seconds or give a massive lump of cookies. Never miss one.
  • Buy upgrades before more buildings: Upgrades that multiply existing building output are almost always better than buying another building. Check the upgrade panel before buying more Grandmas.
  • Milk scales with achievements: Achievements increase Milk percentage, which boosts cookie production when combined with Kitten upgrades. Chasing achievements is the most efficient long-term strategy.
  • Prestige when production slows: The Prestige system resets your run but gives permanent multipliers. Your next run grows exponentially faster. Don't be afraid to reset. It's designed to reward it.
  • Wrinklers are worth keeping: During Grandmapocalypse, wrinklers look like they're hurting production. They're not. Each wrinkler generates 1.1x the cookies it consumes. Pop them when you need a burst.

Made in One Night, Updated for 13 Years

Cookie Clicker was created by Julien "Orteil" Thiennot at DashNet and launched August 8, 2013. Orteil has updated it continuously since.
